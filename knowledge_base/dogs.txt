DOG CARE GUIDE

FEEDING
Adult dogs should be fed twice daily, once in the morning and once in the evening. Puppies under six months need three to four meals per day. The amount of food depends on the dog's size, age, and activity level. Small dogs (under 20 lbs) typically need half to one cup of dry food per meal. Medium dogs (20-50 lbs) need one to two cups per meal. Large dogs (over 50 lbs) need two to four cups per meal. Always provide fresh water throughout the day. Avoid feeding dogs chocolate, grapes, raisins, onions, garlic, xylitol, and macadamia nuts as these are toxic. Table scraps and human food should be avoided or kept to a minimum.

EXERCISE
Dogs require daily exercise to stay healthy and mentally stimulated. Small breeds like Chihuahuas and Yorkies need 20 to 30 minutes of walking per day. Medium breeds like Beagles and Bulldogs need 30 to 60 minutes of daily activity. Large and high-energy breeds like Labrador Retrievers, Golden Retrievers, and Border Collies need 60 to 90 minutes or more of vigorous exercise daily. Exercise should include both physical activity like walking and running and mental stimulation like puzzle toys and training sessions. Puppies should have shorter, more frequent play sessions to avoid overexertion on developing joints.

GROOMING
Short-haired dogs should be brushed once or twice a week. Long-haired dogs require daily brushing to prevent mats and tangles. All dogs need a bath every four to six weeks or when dirty. Nails should be trimmed every three to four weeks. Ears should be checked weekly and cleaned monthly or when visibly dirty. Dental hygiene is important and teeth should be brushed two to three times per week using dog-safe toothpaste.

SLEEP
Adult dogs sleep 12 to 14 hours per day. Puppies and senior dogs may sleep up to 18 hours. Dogs are polyphasic sleepers, meaning they sleep in multiple short periods throughout the day and night.

TRAINING
Dogs thrive on routine and positive reinforcement. Basic obedience training including sit, stay, come, and leash manners should begin early. Puppies should start training at 8 weeks old. Training sessions should be short, 5 to 10 minutes, and fun. Socialization with other dogs and people is critical during the first three months of a puppy's life.

VACCINATION SCHEDULE FOR DOGS
6-8 weeks: Distemper, parvovirus
10-12 weeks: DHPP (distemper, adenovirus, parainfluenza, parvovirus)
16-18 weeks: DHPP booster, rabies
12-16 months: DHPP booster, rabies booster
Every 1-3 years: DHPP and rabies boosters depending on vaccine type and local laws
Optional vaccines: Bordetella (kennel cough), leptospirosis, Lyme disease based on lifestyle and risk

HEALTH MONITORING
Healthy dogs have clear eyes, clean ears, a shiny coat, and pink gums. They should have normal energy levels and a healthy appetite. Watch for signs of illness such as lethargy, vomiting, diarrhea lasting more than 24 hours, loss of appetite for more than a day, excessive thirst or urination, coughing, limping, or changes in behavior. Annual vet visits are recommended for adult dogs and bi-annual visits for seniors over age seven.
