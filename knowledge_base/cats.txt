CAT CARE GUIDE

FEEDING
Adult cats should be fed twice daily. Kittens under six months need three to four small meals per day. Cats are obligate carnivores and require animal protein in their diet. Dry kibble is convenient and good for dental health, while wet food provides additional hydration. Most adult cats need about 200 to 300 calories per day depending on size and activity level. Indoor cats tend to be less active and may need portion control to prevent obesity. Fresh clean water should always be available. Cats often prefer running water, so a pet water fountain may encourage better hydration. Avoid feeding cats onions, garlic, grapes, raisins, alcohol, chocolate, and anything with xylitol.

EXERCISE
Indoor cats need 15 to 30 minutes of active play per day to stay healthy and prevent boredom. Interactive toys like wand toys, laser pointers, and puzzle feeders are excellent for mental and physical stimulation. Cats are most active during dawn and dusk, following their natural hunting instincts. Providing climbing structures like cat trees and window perches encourages natural movement and enrichment. Regular playtime reduces behavioral issues such as scratching furniture and aggression.

GROOMING
Short-haired cats groom themselves effectively but benefit from weekly brushing to reduce shedding and hairballs. Long-haired breeds like Persians and Maine Coons need daily brushing to prevent mats. All cats should have their nails trimmed every two to three weeks. Cats generally do not need baths unless they get into something sticky or smelly. Ears should be checked monthly and cleaned gently if there is visible dark debris. Dental care is important and teeth should be brushed regularly or dental treats provided.

LITTER BOX
The general rule is one litter box per cat plus one extra. Litter boxes should be cleaned daily by scooping and completely emptied and washed once a week. Cats are very sensitive to a dirty litter box and may avoid it if not clean. Place litter boxes in quiet, low-traffic areas of the home. Most cats prefer unscented, clumping litter.

VACCINATION SCHEDULE FOR CATS
6-8 weeks: FVRCP (feline viral rhinotracheitis, calicivirus, panleukopenia)
10-12 weeks: FVRCP booster
14-16 weeks: FVRCP booster, rabies
12 months: FVRCP and rabies boosters
Every 1-3 years: FVRCP and rabies boosters depending on vaccine type
Optional vaccines: FeLV (feline leukemia virus) for cats that go outdoors

SLEEP
Cats are crepuscular and naturally sleep 12 to 16 hours per day. They conserve energy during the day for hunting activity at dawn and dusk. Provide comfortable and safe sleeping spots in multiple locations throughout the home.

HEALTH MONITORING
Healthy cats have clear eyes, a clean coat, pink gums, and a healthy appetite. They should maintain a consistent weight and use the litter box regularly. Warning signs include vomiting or diarrhea lasting more than 24 hours, loss of appetite for more than a day, straining in the litter box especially in male cats, hiding or sudden behavior changes, weight loss, excessive thirst, or discharge from eyes or nose. Annual vet checkups are recommended and bi-annual for cats over age ten.
