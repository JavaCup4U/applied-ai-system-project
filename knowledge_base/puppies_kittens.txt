PUPPY AND KITTEN CARE GUIDE

PUPPIES - FIRST WEEKS AT HOME
Puppies should not leave their mother before eight weeks of age. During the first few days bring the puppy home, keep them in a calm, quiet environment to reduce stress. Establish a consistent routine immediately with regular feeding times, potty breaks, play sessions, and sleep times. Crate training provides puppies with a safe den-like space and helps with housetraining.

PUPPY FEEDING SCHEDULE
8-12 weeks: Four meals per day of puppy-specific food
3-6 months: Three meals per day
6-12 months: Two meals per day transitioning to adult portions
Use high-quality puppy food formulated for the puppy's expected adult size. Small breeds can transition to adult food at 9 to 12 months. Large and giant breeds should stay on puppy food until 12 to 18 months. Overfeeding large breed puppies can contribute to orthopedic problems.

PUPPY EXERCISE GUIDELINES
A general rule for puppy exercise is five minutes per month of age, twice daily. An eight-week-old puppy should have two 10-minute play sessions per day. A four-month-old puppy can handle two 20-minute walks. Avoid high-impact exercise like jumping and running on hard surfaces until growth plates are closed, which occurs between 12 and 18 months depending on breed size. Mental stimulation through training and puzzle toys is as important as physical exercise.

PUPPY SOCIALIZATION WINDOW
The critical socialization period for puppies is three to fourteen weeks. During this time expose puppies to different people, sounds, environments, surfaces, animals, and experiences in a safe and positive way. Puppies who are well socialized early are more confident and less likely to develop fear-based aggression. Puppy classes are an excellent way to socialize and begin training simultaneously.

PUPPY HEALTH MILESTONES
6-8 weeks: First vet visit, first round of vaccinations, fecal test for parasites
10-12 weeks: Second round of vaccines, deworming if needed
14-16 weeks: Third round of vaccines, rabies vaccine
6 months: Discuss spay or neuter with veterinarian
12-18 months: Final puppy vaccines and transition to adult care schedule

KITTENS - FIRST WEEKS AT HOME
Kittens should stay with their mother until at least eight weeks of age. Young kittens need warmth because they cannot regulate their own body temperature until about four weeks. Provide a safe enclosed space for the kitten to explore gradually. Introduce litter boxes immediately as kittens instinctively use them.

KITTEN FEEDING SCHEDULE
6-12 weeks: Four small meals of kitten-specific food per day
3-6 months: Three meals per day
6-12 months: Two meals per day
Kitten food is calorie-dense to support rapid growth. A mix of wet and dry kitten food is ideal. Kittens should transition to adult food at 12 months for average breeds.

KITTEN SOCIALIZATION
Kittens have a socialization window from two to seven weeks. During this time gentle handling by multiple people helps kittens become comfortable with human touch. Expose kittens to different sounds like appliances and visitors to reduce fearfulness. Introduce other pets slowly with controlled supervised meetings.

KITTEN HEALTH MILESTONES
8 weeks: First vet visit and first FVRCP vaccine, fecal test
12 weeks: Second FVRCP vaccine
16 weeks: Third FVRCP vaccine and rabies vaccine
6 months: Discuss spay or neuter with veterinarian
12 months: Final kitten vaccines, transition to adult wellness care

LITTER BOX TRAINING FOR KITTENS
Place kittens in the litter box immediately after meals and naps. Most kittens take to litter boxes naturally. Use low-sided boxes for young kittens so they can climb in easily. Avoid strongly scented litters for kittens. Praise kittens when they use the box correctly.
